Cambridge CAE · C1

Documento persuasivo y orientado al futuro: secciones con headings (Introduction · Current Situation · Suggestions · Expected Benefits · Conclusion), modales tentativos (would/could/might) y conectores de propósito y resultado.

A A 100%
⚠️Atención · examen: este género NO se evalúa en Linguaskill 2025 (ni en la versión General ni en la Business). SÍ es relevante para Cambridge CAE (C1 Advanced, Writing Part 2 opcional). NO aparece en FCE (B2 First). Si tu objetivo es solo el Linguaskill, este tema es de consulta opcional.

Estructura y secciones · Anatomy of a proposal

Una proposal es un documento formal que propone cambios concretos para una situación futura. A diferencia del essay, tiene secciones con headings; a diferencia del report, está orientada al futuro (qué hacer) y no solo al pasado (qué pasó). El tono es persuasivo pero mesurado: nada de imperativos ni de afirmaciones tajantes.

📐
A. Estructura con headings · Five-section blueprint
La organización canónica de la proposal en Cambridge CAE
1
Introduction · objetivo y contexto
1 párrafo corto · presente / future modal · 30–40 palabras

Anuncia el propósito de la propuesta y el contexto que la motiva. No hay tesis aún: solo el marco.

The aim of this proposal is to… This proposal sets out… The purpose of this report is to suggest…
2
Current Situation · diagnóstico
1–2 párrafos · presente / present perfect · 50–70 palabras

Describe el estado actual y por qué hace falta un cambio. Datos, hechos, problemas observados. No es opinión: es descripción del punto de partida.

At present,… Currently, … Recent surveys indicate that… Many users have reported that…
3
Suggestions / Proposals · acciones propuestas
2–3 párrafos o lista numerada · modales tentativos · 80–110 palabras

El corazón del documento: las medidas concretas que propones. Numéralas o usa subheadings. Usa modales would/could/might, nunca imperativos.

I would like to propose that… Firstly, the company could… It would be advisable to… It might be worth considering…
4
Expected Benefits · beneficios previstos
1 párrafo · would + V · 40–60 palabras

Explica qué mejorará si se adoptan las medidas. Sin esta sección, la propuesta queda incompleta: no basta con tener ideas, hay que vender sus efectos.

This would have the benefit of… As a result, … In the long term, this would… Consequently, …
5
Conclusion · llamada a la acción
1 párrafo corto · would + V · 25–35 palabras

Cierra con una recomendación firme pero cortés. Ofrece tu disponibilidad para discutir o ampliar.

For these reasons, I strongly recommend… In conclusion, … I would welcome the opportunity to discuss… I look forward to your consideration.
📏 Total CAE: 220–260 palabras · 5 secciones con headings visibles · tono formal sin contracciones · modales tentativos en lugar de imperativos.

B. Diferencia con el report · Proposal vs Report
Dos géneros con headings, pero objetivos opuestos
🔀Idea central: el report describe lo que ha ocurrido y, al final, recomienda. La proposal propone lo que debería ocurrir desde el principio: las recomendaciones son el contenido, no la conclusión.
Objetivo: Report → informar sobre una situación (qué se observó, qué se midió). Proposal → persuadir para actuar (qué hacer a partir de ahora).
Tiempo verbal dominante: Report → past simple / present perfect / present para describir hallazgos. Proposal → present + would / could / might / should para propuestas hipotéticas y beneficios futuros.
Tono: Report → neutro, objetivo, descriptivo. Proposal → persuasivo pero mesurado — convence sin imponer.
Ubicación de las recomendaciones: Report → al final, como Conclusions and Recommendations. Proposal → desde el principio; ocupan la sección central Suggestions / Proposals.
Secciones típicas: Report → Introduction · Findings · Conclusions · Recommendations. Proposal → Introduction · Current Situation · Suggestions · Expected Benefits · Conclusion. Ambos llevan headings, pero los nombres delatan el foco temporal.

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Errores típicos al pasar del essay al género propuesta
!
NO es un ensayo · tiene secciones con headings visibles

Muchos estudiantes escriben la proposal en bloques sin titular, como si fuera un essay. En Cambridge CAE, los headings (Introduction, Current Situation, Suggestions, Benefits, Conclusion) son obligatorios y el examinador los busca. Sin headings, baja la nota de Communicative Achievement.

!
Modales tentativos · no imperativos, no «you must»

Las sugerencias se formulan con would, could, might, should: «It would be advisable to…», «We could consider…», «I would recommend that…». Imperativos directos («Install new computers») o el must autoritario («The company must do X») rompen el registro y suenan agresivos.

!
Tono persuasivo, NO impositivo

El equilibrio justo es «convencer mostrando ventajas, no forzar». Frases como «It is essential that…», «I strongly recommend…» son firmes pero cortés. Evita el lenguaje absoluto («This is the only solution») o emocional («It would be amazing»). El registro es formal y mesurado.

!
Incluye SIEMPRE los beneficios esperados

Una propuesta sin sección Expected Benefits es media propuesta. No basta con tener ideas; hay que vender sus efectos: ahorro, satisfacción, productividad, imagen, etc. El examinador penaliza la falta de esta sección como «ideas no justificadas».

!
No mezcles pasado (report) con futuro (proposal)

Si dedicas medio texto a describir lo que ya pasó, te estás desviando hacia el report. La sección Current Situation debe ser corta y descriptiva; el grueso del texto va hacia Suggestions y Benefits, ambos en futuro o condicional. Proporción aproximada: 15 % pasado/presente · 85 % futuro/hipotético.

!
Sin contracciones · registro formal completo

I would, do not, would not, it is — nunca I'd, don't, wouldn't, it's. La proposal es un documento institucional, no un email a un colega. Una sola contracción ya rompe el tono. Es el mismo registro del essay y del email formal.

Lenguaje de propuesta · The language toolkit

Tres bloques de lenguaje específicos: modales y estructuras tentativas (para suavizar las sugerencias), frases clave que el examinador reconoce inmediatamente, y conectores de propósito y resultado para hilar la lógica «si hacemos X → conseguimos Y».

🎯Regla central del lenguaje: en una propuesta, lo que NO se enuncia es tan importante como lo que sí. Would, could, might dejan espacio a la negociación; must, will, should always cierran la conversación. El examinador busca el matiz tentativo.

A. Modales y estructuras tentativas
Nueve patrones gramaticales para formular sugerencias formales
Estructura Función Ejemplo
I would suggest + V-ing Proponer (formal, neutral) I would suggest extending opening hours until 10 p.m.
It might be worth + V-ing Sugerencia suave, exploratoria It might be worth considering a flexible working policy.
It would be advisable to + V Recomendar con autoridad cortés It would be advisable to consult staff before any change.
I would recommend that + subj + base V Recomendación formal con subjuntivo I would recommend that the budget be reviewed annually. (no «is reviewed»)
Could we consider + V-ing/N? Pregunta abierta, busca diálogo Could we consider introducing a wellness programme?
If we were to + V, … would + V Hipótesis (2ª condicional) If we were to invest in new equipment, productivity would increase significantly.
This would result in + N Consecuencia prevista This would result in a 20 % reduction in waste.
We should aim to + V Propósito colectivo We should aim to reduce energy consumption by 15 %.
It is essential that + subj + base V Recomendación enfática (subjuntivo) It is essential that all members be informed of the changes in advance.

B. Frases clave por sección · Examiner-valued phrases
Las expresiones que abren cada parte de la proposal
The aim of this proposal is to… apertura clásica · introduce el propósito
This proposal sets out… apertura algo más formal · «este documento expone…»
The purpose of this proposal is to suggest… apertura explícita de sugerencia
Having considered the current situation,… transición de Current Situation → Suggestions
I would like to propose that… introduce la primera sugerencia central
The following measures should be implemented… cuando se introduce una lista numerada
This would have the benefit of + V-ing / N… enlaza una propuesta con su beneficio inmediato
In the long term, this would… para destacar beneficios a largo plazo
For these reasons, I strongly recommend… cierre persuasivo en la conclusión
I look forward to your consideration / I would welcome the opportunity to discuss… despedida cortés y abierta al diálogo

Las palabras que hilan «si hacemos X → conseguimos Y»

  • As a result,
  • This would lead to…
  • In this way,
  • Thereby + V-ing

  • What is more,

E. Modelos · Two annotated proposals
Campus library improvements · Company wellness programme

Model 1 · Proposal for improving the university library

Audience: Student Council · ~255 palabras · Cambridge CAE Writing Part 2
Introduction

The aim of this proposal is to outline a series of practical improvements to the main university library, with a view to enhancing the academic experience of students and supporting longer study sessions during exam periods.

Current Situation

At present, the library opens from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. on weekdays and closes early at weekends. Many students have reported that opening hours are insufficient, particularly during exam weeks, when demand peaks. In addition, the building lacks comfortable seating on the upper floors and access to power outlets is limited.

Suggested Improvements

Having considered student feedback, I would like to propose the following measures. Firstly, opening hours could be extended until midnight from Monday to Friday and the library could remain open on Sundays. Secondly, it would be advisable to install additional power outlets and USB sockets throughout the reading rooms. Finally, I would recommend that a small quiet café area be created on the ground floor for short breaks.

Expected Benefits

These changes would have a number of positive effects. Extended hours would allow students to manage their time more flexibly, while improved facilities would create a more productive study environment. As a result, overall student performance and well-being should significantly improve.

Conclusion

For these reasons, I strongly recommend that the proposed measures be adopted before the start of the next academic year. I look forward to your consideration and remain at your disposal for any further discussion.

Model 2 · Proposal for introducing a company wellness programme

Audience: Board of Directors · ~275 palabras · formal corporate register
Introduction

This proposal sets out a framework for implementing a comprehensive wellness programme aimed at improving employee health, motivation and long-term retention within the company.

Current Situation

Recent internal surveys indicate that nearly 40 % of staff report high levels of stress, while sick-leave figures have risen by 15 % over the past year. Although the company offers competitive salaries, very little is currently in place to support physical and mental well-being. This situation, if left unaddressed, may continue to affect productivity and increase recruitment costs.

Proposed Measures

Having reviewed best practices in the sector, I would like to propose three concrete initiatives. Firstly, the company could subsidise gym memberships and offer weekly yoga sessions on the premises. Secondly, it might be worth introducing a confidential employee assistance programme with access to qualified counsellors. Finally, I would recommend that flexible working arrangements be expanded to include two remote days per week.

Anticipated Benefits

These measures would yield clear advantages. Healthier and more rested employees tend to demonstrate greater focus, creativity and loyalty. Consequently, the company would benefit from reduced absenteeism, lower turnover and a stronger reputation as an employer of choice in a competitive market.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the proposed programme would represent a meaningful investment in the workforce and, in the long term, in the success of the company itself. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss these suggestions further with the board.

Email Formal · Formal Email

El email formal se usa para quejas, solicitudes, consultas oficiales y aplicaciones laborales. Mantén un registro impersonal, evita contracciones (I'm → I am) y estructura el mensaje en párrafos claros: apertura, motivo, detalle, petición, cierre.

E. Ideal Structure · Estructura ideal
Esquema paso a paso que funciona para cualquier email formal
1
Saludo formal · Greeting

Identifica al destinatario con un saludo adecuado al tipo de email.

Dear Sir/Madam, Dear Mr/Ms Smith, To Whom It May Concern,
2
Apertura / motivo · Opening line

Explica desde la primera línea por qué escribes — sé directo y específico.

I am writing to enquire about… I am writing with reference to… Further to your recent…
3
Desarrollo / detalle · Body

Aporta el contexto, los datos concretos o el problema. Un párrafo por idea, con conectores formales.

Furthermore, In addition, However, Despite this,
4
Petición · Request

Pide claramente lo que necesitas: información, acción, respuesta o compensación.

I would be grateful if you could… Could you please clarify…? I would appreciate it if…
5
Cierre cortés · Polite closing

Ofrece disponibilidad para seguir en contacto y agradece el tiempo del lector.

I look forward to hearing from you. Please do not hesitate to contact me. Thank you for your time.
6
Despedida + firma · Sign-off

Empareja la despedida con el saludo: Dear Sir/Madam → Yours faithfully; con nombre → Yours sincerely.

Yours faithfully, Yours sincerely, Kind regards,

G. Tips & Common Pitfalls
Consejos prácticos y errores frecuentes
✓
Evita las contracciones

Escribe siempre la forma completa: I am writing (no I'm writing), do not hesitate (no don't hesitate). Las contracciones son el primer indicador de registro informal.

✓
Empieza con el motivo

En el primer párrafo, explica claramente por qué escribes: I am writing to enquire about…, I am writing with reference to…, I am writing to complain about…

✓
Empareja saludo y despedida

Dear Sir/Madam → Yours faithfully. Dear Mr Smith → Yours sincerely. Nunca los mezcles: es un error que el examinador detecta inmediatamente.

✓
Estructura cuidada en párrafos cortos

Un párrafo por idea: apertura, motivo/problema, desarrollo o petición, cierre. No mezcles ideas distintas en el mismo párrafo.

!
Evita el lenguaje coloquial

Nada de stuff, guys, thing, really nice, OK. Sustituye por items, colleagues, matter, satisfactory, acceptable.

!
Evita las exclamaciones

Mantén un tono mesurado y profesional. Una sola "!" puede romper el registro formal de todo el email.

El email informal va dirigido a amigos, familiares o conocidos cercanos. Usa contracciones (I'm, don't, can't), expresiones coloquiales, exclamaciones y un tono cercano. La estructura es más libre, pero conviene mantener apertura, contenido y cierre claros.

G. Tips & Common Pitfalls
Consejos prácticos y errores frecuentes
✓
Usa contracciones libremente

I'm, don't, won't, it's, I'd suenan naturales y son lo que se espera en un email amistoso. Sin contracciones, el texto suena rígido.

✓
Saluda con calidez antes del tema

Pregunta cómo está la persona antes de entrar en materia: Hope you're doing well!, How have you been?, What have you been up to?

✓
Cierra con cercanía

Take care!, Lots of love,, Cheers,, Chat soon! — nunca Yours sincerely en un email a un amigo: rompe el registro.

✓
Usa exclamaciones e interjecciones

Refuerzan el tono cercano: Honestly!, Guess what!, You won't believe…! Pero sin exagerar — una o dos por email son suficientes.

!
No abuses del slang muy local

En B2 no necesitas expresiones regionales que el examinador pueda no reconocer. Lo idiomático estándar (gutted, mate, loads) sí; lo demasiado coloquial, no.

!
Mantén una estructura clara

Aunque sea informal, ordena: apertura → contenido (uno o dos párrafos temáticos) → cierre. Un email sin estructura parece desordenado y baja la nota.

Ensayo Discursivo · Discursive Essay

El ensayo B2 puede ser de opinión, a favor y en contra (for & against) o discursivo. Estructura clásica de 4 párrafos: introducción, argumentos a favor, argumentos en contra (o contraargumento), y conclusión. Mantén el registro académico: evita contracciones y opiniones demasiado coloquiales.

💡Tip Linguaskill B2: los examinadores valoran variedad léxica y cohesión. Una buena introducción y una conclusión equilibrada suben mucho la nota.
👋

Model 1 · For & Against Essay

Should young people be required to do voluntary work in their community? · ~225 words

IntroductionIn today's world, it is widely accepted that young people should play an active role in their communities. The question of whether voluntary work should be made compulsory for them, however, has become increasingly relevant, with strong arguments on both sides.

Arguments ForOn the one hand, those who support mandatory community service argue that it offers significant personal benefits. Furthermore, volunteering helps young people develop key skills such as teamwork, responsibility and empathy. A clear example of this is the well-documented success of school programmes in countries like Germany, where students who participate report higher self-esteem and stronger civic engagement.

Arguments AgainstOn the other hand, critics claim that forcing teenagers to volunteer defeats the very purpose of voluntary work. If participation is not voluntary, the experience may feel like punishment rather than contribution. Moreover, many young people already face considerable academic pressure, and adding compulsory hours could harm their studies and their mental health.

ConclusionHaving considered both sides of the argument, I believe that voluntary work should be strongly encouraged but never imposed. Schools could offer recognition, credits or rewards for participation, allowing students to choose freely. Ultimately, genuine commitment is far more valuable than reluctant compliance.

Model 2 · Opinion Essay

"Social media has done more harm than good." Discuss. · ~230 words

IntroductionOver recent decades, social media has become a matter of growing concern. Platforms like Instagram, TikTok and X have transformed the way we communicate, but it is widely argued that they have done more harm than good. While there are valid points on both sides, I would argue that I tend to agree with this view.

My ViewFirst and foremost, social media has had a profound impact on mental health, particularly among teenagers. The evidence suggests that excessive use is linked to anxiety, low self-esteem and sleep problems. In addition, the spread of misinformation has become alarming: fake news can travel faster than verified facts, influencing elections and damaging public trust in institutions.

Counter + RebuttalAdmittedly, social media has clear benefits. It allows families separated by thousands of kilometres to stay in touch and gives small businesses a free platform to reach new customers. However, these advantages do not outweigh the damage caused by addictive algorithms specifically designed to keep users scrolling for hours at a time.

ConclusionTo conclude, while social media offers undeniable opportunities for connection, the evidence suggests that its negative consequences are far greater. It is clear that further action is needed, both from users and from regulators, to ensure these platforms serve us rather than harm us.

Model 3 · For & Against Essay

Should public transport be free in major cities? · ~230 words

IntroductionOver recent decades, urban transport has become a matter of growing concern. The question of whether public transport should be made free in major cities has become increasingly relevant, with strong arguments on both sides.

Arguments ForSupporters of free public transport argue that it would offer clear environmental and social benefits. Furthermore, removing fares would encourage millions of commuters to leave their cars at home, reducing pollution and congestion. A clear example of this is Luxembourg, where free buses and trains have led to a noticeable drop in private vehicle use. In addition, free transport would benefit low-income families who currently spend a significant portion of their income on travel.

Arguments AgainstOn the other hand, critics claim that the policy would be extremely expensive and difficult to sustain. The lost revenue would have to be recovered through taxation, which is often unpopular. Moreover, free services tend to suffer from overcrowding and reduced quality, as can be seen in some pilot schemes in Estonia.

ConclusionHaving considered both sides, I believe that free public transport is a desirable goal, but only if combined with sustainable funding and proper investment in capacity. Ultimately, the decision rests with city governments willing to balance ambition with reality.

Model 4 · Opinion Essay

Is studying abroad worth the high cost? Discuss. · ~240 words

IntroductionIn today's world, it is widely accepted that international experience is highly valuable. The question of whether studying abroad is worth its considerable cost, however, has become increasingly relevant, with universities charging higher fees every year. Personally, I believe the benefits clearly outweigh the financial sacrifice.

My ViewFirst and foremost, studying abroad transforms students both academically and personally. Living in a foreign country forces young people to become independent, adaptable and resilient. Moreover, learning in a different educational system exposes them to new methods and perspectives. The evidence suggests that graduates who have studied abroad earn higher salaries and are far more likely to be hired by international companies.

Counter + RebuttalAdmittedly, the financial burden is significant, and not every family can afford it. However, this argument overlooks the wide range of scholarships, exchange programmes and student loans that make these experiences accessible. Furthermore, the long-term return — in skills, languages and opportunities — far exceeds the initial cost.

ConclusionTaking everything into account, it seems clear that studying abroad is one of the most valuable investments a young person can make. It is clear that further support is needed so that more students can access this life-changing opportunity.

Model 5 · For & Against Essay

Should governments ban single-use plastics completely? · ~215 words

IntroductionOver recent decades, plastic pollution has become a matter of growing concern. The question of whether governments should impose a complete ban on single-use plastics has become increasingly relevant, with strong arguments on both sides.

Arguments ForSupporters of a total ban argue that the environmental benefits would be enormous. Furthermore, single-use plastics take centuries to decompose and are now found in oceans, soil and even drinking water. A clear example of this is Kenya, where strict laws against plastic bags have led to noticeable improvements in urban cleanliness. In addition, a ban would force companies to invest in biodegradable alternatives, accelerating innovation.

Arguments AgainstOn the other hand, critics claim that an immediate ban would be impractical and economically damaging. Many sectors — from healthcare to food packaging — currently rely on plastic for hygiene and safety reasons. Moreover, viable alternatives remain expensive, which would disproportionately affect low-income consumers and small businesses.

ConclusionHaving considered both sides, I believe that a phased ban combined with public investment in alternatives is the most realistic path forward. Ultimately, the urgency of the environmental crisis leaves us with no real choice but to act decisively.

Model 6 · Opinion Essay

"Working from home is better than working in an office." Discuss. · ~235 words

IntroductionOver recent decades, the way we work has been transformed by digital technology. The question of whether working from home is better than working in a traditional office has become increasingly relevant, especially since the pandemic. While there are valid points on both sides, I tend to agree that home-based work offers more advantages than disadvantages.

My ViewFirst and foremost, working from home grants employees a degree of flexibility and autonomy that no office environment can match. The evidence suggests that remote workers tend to be more productive and report higher levels of job satisfaction. Furthermore, eliminating the daily commute saves both time and money, while also reducing pollution caused by traffic in major cities.

Counter + RebuttalAdmittedly, working from home can blur the line between professional and personal life, and some employees miss the spontaneous interaction with colleagues. However, these issues can be addressed with clear schedules and regular team meetings online. In addition, hybrid models allow workers to enjoy the best of both worlds.

ConclusionTo conclude, while the office still has its merits, the evidence suggests that working from home offers greater flexibility, productivity and quality of life. It is clear that further investment is needed in remote-work culture if companies wish to attract and retain talent.

G. Tips & Common Pitfalls
Consejos prácticos y errores frecuentes
✓
Varía los conectores

Alterna However con Nevertheless o On the other hand. No repitas el mismo dos veces seguidas: el examinador valora la variedad léxica más que la corrección de un conector usado en bucle.

✓
Usa hedging académico

Suaviza afirmaciones con tend to, may, It could be argued that…, arguably. Aporta el tono mesurado y académico que se espera en B2/C1.

✓
Da ejemplos concretos

Un For instance, in Germany… con un dato real sube más la nota que tres afirmaciones generales. Concreta con países, datos, estudios o casos específicos.

✓
Estructura clara de 4 párrafos

Introducción + a favor + en contra + conclusión. O bien: introducción + tu opinión con argumentos + contraargumento con refutación + conclusión. Sin estructura, los puntos se pierden.

!
Evita las contracciones

En un ensayo académico, escribe do not, it is, cannot — no don't, it's, can't. Las contracciones bajan el registro inmediatamente.

!
No abuses de "I think"

Sustitúyelo por In my view, It could be argued that, From my perspective, There is a strong case for. Demuestra registro académico y variedad.

!
La conclusión no es un resumen

Posiciónate, cierra con una idea fuerte o una reflexión final. No repitas literalmente lo que ya has dicho: añade una valoración o una mirada al futuro.

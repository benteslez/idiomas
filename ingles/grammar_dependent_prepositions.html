Verbo + preposición · Verb + dependent preposition

Algunos verbos exigen una preposición concreta detrás (depend on, no depend of). No hay reglas lógicas: cada verbo se aprende junto a su preposición como una unidad léxica. Es la fuente número uno de errores en huecos de Reading y un detalle clave para la precisión en Writing.

📊Relevancia Linguaskill: altísima — los huecos de Reading Parts 2 y 4 piden preposición correcta sin parar; en Writing, una sola preposición errónea baja la marca de Lexical Accuracy. Aprender el verbo siempre con su preposición es el ROI más alto de tu tiempo de estudio.
🟢
A. Verbos con ON / IN / OF
Las tres preposiciones que rigen más verbos a nivel B2–C1.
ONapoyo, dependencia, foco

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Donde el español induce la preposición equivocada.
!
depend ON, no depend of

✗ It depends of you. ✓ It depends on you. El español "depender de" arrastra a usar of, pero el inglés usa on sin excepciones. Lo mismo con el sustantivo: dependence on (no of).

!
think OF / ABOUT, no in

✗ I'm thinking in you. ✓ I'm thinking about you (a la mente cariñosa) / I'm thinking of changing jobs (considerando). El español "pensar en" induce in, pero nunca es correcto.

!
consist OF, no of of ni from

✗ The team consists from five people. ✓ The team consists of five people. Para el resultado: The team is made up of five people. Pero nunca consist from.

!
arrive AT / IN, no to

✗ We arrived to Madrid. ✓ We arrived in Madrid (ciudad, país) / We arrived at the station (punto concreto). El español "llegar a" se calca como to, pero arrive to no existe.

!
listen TO, no preposición omitida

✗ Listen the music. ✓ Listen to the music. En español "escuchar la música" no lleva preposición — pero listen SIEMPRE va seguido de to cuando hay objeto. Igual con reply to, respond to.

!
explain TO sb, no explain sb

✗ Can you explain me the rules?. ✓ Can you explain the rules to me?. El verbo explain no admite objeto indirecto sin to. Lo mismo con describe to, suggest to, mention to.

!
agree: WITH sb / ON sth / TO + V

Tres preposiciones según el complemento: I agree with you (persona) / We agreed on a plan (decisión) / She agreed to help (acción). Aprende cada combinación con su contexto.

Model 1 · Apology email to a colleague

Style: professional semi-formal · ~155 words · 9 verb+prep combinations

Dear Andrea,

I'm writing to apologise for missing yesterday's meeting. I had to deal with an unexpected family issue and I completely forgot to send you a message — I do hope you'll forgive me. Marta

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Los adjetivos más calcados del español.
!
married TO, no with

✗ She's married with a doctor (eso significaría "casada con un doctor a su lado"). ✓ She's married to a doctor. El español "casado con" induce with, pero la unión matrimonial en inglés se expresa con to.

!
good AT, no in

✗ I'm good in maths. ✓ I'm good at maths. Lo mismo con bad at, brilliant at, terrible at, hopeless at. El "bueno en" español induce in, pero la habilidad en inglés se mide con at.

!
afraid OF, no from / to

✗ I'm afraid from spiders. ✓ I'm afraid of spiders. Lo mismo con scared of, frightened of, terrified of. El miedo en inglés se construye con of, sin excepciones.

!
interested IN, no for / on

✗ I'm interested for photography. ✓ I'm interested in photography. El sustantivo paralelo también: interest in (ver pestaña 3).

!
different FROM (UK), different than (US)

✗ Different of / to the original en escritura formal británica. ✓ Different from the original (estándar UK) / Different than the original (US). En examen Linguaskill (registro británico), opta por from.

!
responsible FOR, no of

✗ I'm responsible of the team. ✓ I'm responsible for the team. El español "responsable de" arrastra a of, pero la responsabilidad en inglés siempre va con for. Lo mismo con el sustantivo: responsibility for.

!
angry / annoyed / upset: la preposición depende del objeto

Angry with sb (persona) pero angry about sth (situación). Disappointed with sth / in sb. Worried about (siempre). Memoriza cada par con su contexto típico.

E. En contexto · Adj + prep in real prose
Reflexión personal con 9 combinaciones adjetivo + preposición resaltadas.

Model 1 · On working in small teams

Style: personal opinion piece · ~150 words · 9 adj+prep combinations

From my own experience, I'm convinced that working in a small team is far more rewarding than being part of a huge organisation. In big companies you can easily become invisible: you may be responsible for an important project, but rarely feel proud of the result. Smaller teams, by contrast, tend to be familiar with each individual's strengths and weaknesses, and people are genuinely interested in one another's progress.

Of course, small teams have their downsides. You may be exposed to too many responsibilities at once, and some colleagues are less suited to that kind of intensity. Still, if you're capable of handling pressure and you're not afraid of multitasking, the rewards far outweigh the difficulties. I, for one, am enthusiastic about the model and grateful for the freedom it provides.

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Sustantivos que NO heredan la preposición del español.
!
reason FOR, no of

✗ The reason of the delay. ✓ The reason for the delay. El español "la razón de" induce of, pero el inglés exige for (propósito/motivo). El verbo paralelo cambia: I called to explain (para + V), pero el sustantivo reason for.

!
effect / impact ON, no in

✗ The effect in the economy. ✓ The effect on the economy. Igual con impact on, influence on. El verbo paralelo: to affect / to impact (on).

!
solution / answer TO, no of / for

✗ The solution of / for the problem. ✓ The solution to the problem. Lo mismo con answer to, key to, response to. El "destino" mental de la respuesta va con to.

!
difference BETWEEN, no of / among

✗ The difference of / among the two options. ✓ The difference between the two options. Between incluso con tres o más en este caso (cuando se trata de comparaciones distintas, una a una). Among se usa para "rodeado de".

!
relationship WITH sb / BETWEEN A and B

Dos preposiciones según el formato: My relationship with my sister (un lado de la pareja) vs The relationship between the two countries (vínculo bidireccional explícito). ✗ Relationship of.

!
increase / decrease / rise / fall IN, no of

✗ An increase of sales (eso significa "un aumento de tal cantidad"). ✓ An increase in sales (= en el área de). Of sí se usa para la cantidad: An increase of 10% in sales.

!
Conexión con tema 30 (verbos+prep) y tema 17 (adj+prep)

La familia léxica suele heredar la preposición: interested in → interest in; responsible for → responsibility for. Pero hay excepciones notorias: depend on → dependence on (igual), pero insist on → insistence on (igual). Comprueba siempre el sustantivo aparte.

Model 1 · The remote work revolution

Style: analytical opinion piece · ~155 words · 10 noun+prep combinations

There is no single reason for the recent rise in remote work; instead, it's the combination of several factors. Surveys consistently report an increase in employees' satisfaction with hybrid arrangements, and many cite improved access to family time as a major benefit. The link between flexibility and productivity is widely accepted, although evidence on the long-term effect on team cohesion remains mixed.

For employers, the challenge lies in finding a sensible solution to the loss of informal interaction. Some have responded with creative answers, including monthly in-person retreats and shared online spaces. Whatever the approach, the relationship between trust and autonomy will continue to define the future of work — and our collective attitude to the office as we know it.

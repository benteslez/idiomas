Estructura y formato · Structure & Format

Un report es un texto objetivo y estructurado que presenta datos, hallazgos y recomendaciones sobre un tema concreto. A diferencia del essay, NO defiende una postura personal: describe la realidad de forma neutra y cuantificada, organizada en secciones con headings claros. La opinión solo aparece (matizada) en las Recommendations.

📐
A. Estructura con headings · The five-block layout
Title → Introduction → Findings → Conclusion → Recommendations
1
Title · Título
1 línea · informativo y neutro

Un título claro y descriptivo que resume el tema y el periodo, no una pregunta retórica.

Customer Satisfaction Survey — Q4 2025 Report on Remote Work Preferences Annual Sales Performance Review
2
Introduction · Introducción
2–3 frases · ~30–40 palabras

Indica el propósito (aim), el alcance (scope) y la fuente de los datos. Sin opinión.

The aim of this report is to… The purpose of this report is to… The data presented are based on…
3
Findings · Hallazgos
2 subsecciones con subheadings · ~120 palabras

Presenta los datos en 1–2 bloques con subheadings propios. Pasiva impersonal, porcentajes concretos, comparaciones objetivas. Nada de "I think".

It was found that… The majority of respondents… A significant proportion (X %)… As can be seen in figure 1…
4
Conclusion · Conclusión
1 párrafo corto · ~30–40 palabras

Resume los hallazgos clave de manera imparcial. NO introduzcas datos nuevos ni opiniones aún.

Based on the findings,… The evidence presented suggests that… In summary, the report has shown that…
5
Recommendations · Recomendaciones
2–3 puntos con bullets o frases · ~40–60 palabras

Único bloque donde aparece tu juicio, siempre matizado con modales (no imperativos). Habitualmente en lista.

It is recommended that… It would be advisable to… A possible course of action would be to…
📏 Total: 5 bloques + 1–2 subheadings · 220–260 palabras · headings en negrita o numerados · sin contracciones

B. Formato · Layout & visual rules
Lo que distingue un report de un ensayo a simple vista
Headings clarosclear headings
  • Cada sección con su heading en negrita o numerado (1. Introduction…).
  • Los subheadings dentro de Findings ayudan a navegar.
  • No al texto corrido sin etiquetas.
Párrafos cortosshort paragraphs
  • 3–5 líneas por párrafo.
  • Una idea principal cuantificada por párrafo.
  • Aire visual entre bloques.
Datos cuantificadosdata-driven
  • Porcentajes, cifras absolutas y comparativas.
  • 68 % of respondents…, a rise of 12 points…
  • Datos antes que adjetivos.
Sin opinión personalno "I think"
  • En Findings y Conclusion: cero opinión.
  • La opinión vive solo en Recommendations y va matizada.
  • Nada de "I believe / In my opinion".
Pasiva impersonalimpersonal voice

Bullets cuando ayudanbullet points

  • También listas de cifras paralelas.
  • Bullets cortos, paralelos en estructura.

  • do not, it is, cannot — nunca don't, it's, can't.

  • Sin exclamaciones, sin preguntas retóricas.

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Los errores que delatan al candidato que escribe un informe como si fuese un ensayo
!
El report NO es un ensayo

El informe describe y recomienda; el ensayo argumenta. NO incluyas "In my opinion" ni "I think" en Findings ni en Conclusion. La opinión vive solo en Recommendations y aparece matizada (It would be advisable to…).

!
Headings obligatorios — no bloques de texto corrido

El informe en español puede aceptar prosa continua; el inglés exige secciones etiquetadas: Introduction, Findings, Conclusion, Recommendations. Sin headings, el examinador resta directamente: tu texto parece un essay disfrazado.

!
Pasiva impersonal, no "we found"

Calco típico: "We found that…", "We noticed that…". Reescribe en pasiva: ✓ It was found that…, ✓ It was observed that…, ✓ The data show that…. El sujeto son los datos, no el redactor.

!
Recomendaciones con modales, no con imperativos

El español tolera el imperativo ("Mejore la logística"); el informe inglés prefiere modales y estructuras impersonales: ✓ It is recommended that the company improve its logistics. ✓ It would be advisable to invest in… ✓ The company should consider + V-ing.

!
Datos antes que adjetivos

El informe español a veces se permite "resultados sorprendentes" sin cifras. El inglés exige el dato concreto: "results rose by 12 percentage points", no "results increased a lot". La cuantificación es lo que da credibilidad.

!
No uses contracciones

I'm, don't, won't, it's bajan el registro inmediatamente. Escribe siempre las formas completas. Es una de las pistas más rápidas que usa el examinador para detectar registro inadecuado.

!
El formato español es más libre — el inglés no

El informe en español permite cierta libertad estructural. En B2/C1 inglés (especialmente en Linguaskill Business y Cambridge), las 4–5 secciones son innegociables. Si la consigna pide "a report", espera Title + Intro + Findings + Conclusion + Recommendations.

Lenguaje del informe · Report Language

El lenguaje del informe es objetivo, impersonal y técnico. Tres recursos lo construyen: pasiva impersonal (It was found that…), nominalización (the improvement of…) y frases prefabricadas propias de cada sección. Sin contracciones, sin opiniones personales hasta Recommendations.

E. Reports modelo · Two complete examples
~250 palabras · 5 bloques · headings + frases resaltadas

Model 1 · Workplace Hybrid Work Preferences Survey

Linguaskill Business · ~255 words · 5 sections

TitleWorkplace Hybrid Work Preferences Survey — March 2026

IntroductionThe purpose of this report is to examine employee preferences regarding hybrid working arrangements at TechSolutions Ltd. The data presented are based on an internal survey conducted between January and February 2026, with 450 respondents from all departments.

Findings — Preferred patternIt was found that 68 % of respondents favour a hybrid model of three days in the office and two days remote. A further 22 % prefer a fully remote arrangement, while only 10 % would choose full-time office work. The marketing and IT departments showed the strongest preference for remote work.

Findings — Reported challengesA significant proportion of respondents (54 %) reported difficulties with collaboration when working remotely. In addition, 41 % mentioned issues with work-life balance. As shown by the data, employees with children at home were notably more affected.

ConclusionBased on the findings, there is a clear preference for hybrid working among employees at all levels. However, the data also indicate that collaboration and well-being need to be addressed if remote arrangements are to remain effective.

Recommendation 1It is recommended that the company formalise a three-two hybrid policy for most departments.

Recommendation 2It would be advisable to invest in collaboration tools and provide additional training in remote teamwork.

Recommendation 3A possible course of action would be to introduce flexible scheduling for employees with caring responsibilities.

Model 2 · Customer Satisfaction Survey — Q4 2025

Linguaskill Business · ~260 words · 5 sections

TitleCustomer Satisfaction Survey — Q4 2025

IntroductionThe aim of this report is to provide an overview of customer satisfaction levels at Brightline Retail during the fourth quarter of 2025. The findings are based on 1,200 online reviews and a follow-up questionnaire sent to a random sample of 300 customers.

Findings — Overall satisfactionThe data show that 72 % of customers rated their experience as "good" or "excellent", which represents a slight improvement compared with Q3. A noticeable trend is that satisfaction with the website's checkout process rose by 8 percentage points.

Findings — Areas of concernIt was found that delivery times remain the main source of complaints, with 28 % of respondents reporting delays of more than five working days. In particular, complaints about communication during delivery were significantly higher among first-time customers.

ConclusionThe evidence presented suggests that customer satisfaction has improved overall, although delivery performance continues to undermine the company's reputation. The data also indicate that first-time buyers form the most vulnerable segment.

Recommendation 1It is recommended that the company review its logistics partnerships in order to reduce delivery delays.

Recommendation 2It would be advisable to introduce automated delivery updates to keep customers informed in real time.

Recommendation 3A possible course of action would be to launch a dedicated onboarding email series for first-time customers.

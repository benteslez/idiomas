🔁 Passive Voice · Voz pasiva

Linguaskill B2 · B2–C1

Foco en la acción, no en el agente · be + participio en todos los tiempos · causative (have/get done) · reporting verbs en pasiva

A A 100%

Pasiva en todos los tiempos · Passive in every tense

La voz pasiva se forma con be (en el tiempo de la activa) + participio pasado (V3). Pone el foco en el objeto o la acción, no en quién la realiza. Se usa cuando el agente es desconocido, irrelevante u obvio, y aporta el tono impersonal y formal típico de noticias, informes y descripciones de procesos.

📊Relevancia Linguaskill: aparece constantemente en Reading & Listening (titulares, descripciones de procesos, anuncios oficiales); aporta registro académico al essay (Writing P2); imprescindible en el módulo Business para redactar reports impersonales.
🔧
A. Forma por tiempo · Form in every tense
Misma información, foco distinto. Conjuga be en el tiempo correspondiente + V3.
Activa → Pasiva en los principales tiempos verbales
TiempoActivaPasivaEjemplo
Present Simple S + V(s) S + am/is/are + V3 They make these phones in Korea. → These phones are made in Korea.
Estos teléfonos se fabrican en Corea.
Present Continuous S + am/is/are + V-ing S + am/is/are + being + V3 They are building a new bridge. → A new bridge is being built.
Se está construyendo un nuevo puente.
Past Simple S + V2 S + was/were + V3 Picasso painted Guernica in 1937. → Guernica was painted by Picasso in 1937.
El Guernica fue pintado por Picasso en 1937.
Past Continuous S + was/were + V-ing S + was/were + being + V3 They were cleaning the room when I arrived. → The room was being cleaned when I arrived.
La habitación se estaba limpiando cuando llegué.
Present Perfect S + have/has + V3 S + have/has + been + V3 Someone has eaten my sandwich. → My sandwich has been eaten.
Se han comido mi sándwich.
Past Perfect S + had + V3 S + had + been + V3 She realised that someone had stolen her wallet. → She realised that her wallet had been stolen.
Se dio cuenta de que le habían robado la cartera.
Future (will) S + will + V S + will be + V3 They will announce the winner tomorrow. → The winner will be announced tomorrow.
El ganador será anunciado mañana.
Future Perfect S + will have + V3 S + will have been + V3 They will have finished the road by July. → The road will have been finished by July.
La carretera habrá quedado terminada para julio.
Be going to S + am/is/are going to + V S + am/is/are going to be + V3 They are going to release a new album. → A new album is going to be released.
Va a salir un nuevo álbum.
Modals (present) S + modal + V S + modal + be + V3 You must wear a helmet. → A helmet must be worn.
Debe llevarse casco.
Modal perfect (past) S + modal + have + V3 S + modal + have been + V3 Someone must have taken it. → It must have been taken.
Alguien debe de haberlo cogido.
Infinitive / -ing to + V / V-ing to be + V3 / being + V3 No one likes to be criticised. · She hates being interrupted.
A nadie le gusta que lo critiquen / que la interrumpan.

B. Uso · When to choose the passive
Cuatro motivos para elegir la pasiva en lugar de la activa.
Agente desconocido o irrelevanteunknown / irrelevant agent
  • My bike has been stolen. (no sabemos quién)
  • The window was broken last night. (no importa quién)
  • English is spoken here. (la gente en general)
Foco en la acción o el objetofocus on action / object
  • The Mona Lisa was painted in the early 1500s. (el cuadro es el tema)
  • Penicillin was discovered by Fleming. (la sustancia importa más que el científico)
Registro formal e impersonalformal / impersonal tone
  • The report will be submitted by Friday. (informes, instrucciones)
  • Visitors are kindly requested to remain seated. (avisos oficiales)
  • It has been decided that… (decisiones colectivas)
by + agente: sólo si aportaby + agent: only if it adds info
  • Hamlet was written by Shakespeare. (añade información clave)
  • The cake was eaten. (no decimos "by someone": evidente)
  • The package was delivered by DHL. (la empresa concreta importa)

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes · Spanish-speaker pitfalls
Interferencias del español que hacen perder marca.
!
Pasiva refleja del español (se)

El español prefiere se construyó en 1990; el inglés usa pasiva directa: It was built in 1990. ✗ It was constructed itself / It built itself in 1990. Lo mismo con se venden pisos → Flats are sold (no they sell themselves).

!
Sobreusar la pasiva en el essay

Un toque de pasiva sube el registro; un essay 100 % en pasiva suena artificial y pesado. Regla: 2–4 pasivas bien colocadas en 180 palabras son suficientes para que el examinador la marque.

!
Participio correcto (V3), no infinitivo ni V2

✗ was build / was buyed / was eat. ✓ was built, was bought, was eaten. Repasa la lista de irregulares: en pasiva la tercera columna es obligatoria.

!
Causative vs reflexivo: I cut my hair ≠ I had my hair cut

I cut my hair = yo me lo corté (yo, con tijeras). I had / got my hair cut = fui a la peluquería. El español suele decir "me corté el pelo" para ambas — en inglés son frases distintas. Cuidado también con I painted my house (yo cogí el rodillo) vs I had my house painted (contraté a alguien).

!
Born es siempre pasivo

Nadie "se nace" en inglés: ✓ I was born in 1992. ✗ I born in 1992 / I am born in 1992. La forma activa bear existe (she bore a son) pero es de registro literario; en lenguaje normal, siempre pasiva.

!
"By" no equivale siempre a "por"

Sólo se usa by para el agente de la pasiva: written by Shakespeare. Para "por" causal, usa because of / due to: ✗ The flight was cancelled by the storm → ✓ The flight was cancelled because of the storm (o due to).

Causative · have / get something done

El causative se usa cuando no haces tú la acción, sino que alguien la hace para ti (normalmente porque le pagas o se lo encargas). Es muy frecuente en inglés cotidiano —servicios, reparaciones, peluquería— y un calco que los hispanohablantes suelen evitar por desconocimiento.

💡Regla rápida: si la acción la realiza otra persona para ti, usa have/get + objeto + participio. Si la haces tú mismo, usa la activa normal. Compara: I cut my hair (yo, con tijeras) vs I had my hair cut (en la peluquería).
🔧
A. Forma · Causative structures
Dos familias: have/get something done (encargar un servicio) y have/get someone do something (encargar a una persona).
Causative — estructura, significado y ejemplo
EstructuraSignificadoEjemplo
have + objeto + V3
(past participle)		 Encargar/contratar a alguien para que haga algo por ti. Registro neutro-formal. I had my car repaired last week.
Llevé el coche a reparar la semana pasada.
get + objeto + V3
(past participle)		 Mismo significado que have done, pero más coloquial y frecuente en conversación. I need to get my hair cut before the interview.
Tengo que cortarme el pelo (en la peluquería) antes de la entrevista.
have + objeto + V3
(experiencia negativa)		 Cuando algo malo te ocurre — no lo provocas, lo padeces. Sentido pasivo de "sufrir". She had her bag stolen on the metro.
Le robaron el bolso en el metro.
have + someone + V
(bare infinitive)		 Dar instrucciones / ordenar a una persona concreta que haga algo. Tono más directivo. I'll have the technician check the boiler.
Haré que el técnico revise la caldera.
get + someone + to + V
(to-infinitive)		 Convencer/conseguir que alguien haga algo (a veces con esfuerzo). Más coloquial. I finally got my brother to help me move house.
Por fin conseguí que mi hermano me ayudara con la mudanza.
Todos los tiempos
(have/get se conjuga)		 El objeto y el V3 no cambian; sólo cambia el tiempo de have o get. I'm having my flat painted. · I've had my eyes tested. · I'll get the report translated.
Me están pintando el piso. · Me han revisado la vista. · Haré que traduzcan el informe.

C. Ejemplos · Causative in context
Frases cotidianas con causative — servicios, accidentes, encargos.
I had my eyes tested last month and I need new glasses. Me revisé la vista el mes pasado y necesito gafas nuevas.
We're getting the kitchen redone in July. Nos van a reformar la cocina en julio.
You should have your laptop repaired instead of buying a new one. Deberías llevar el portátil a reparar en lugar de comprar otro.
He had his nose broken playing rugby. Le rompieron la nariz jugando al rugby.
I'm having the documents translated by a sworn translator. Estoy haciendo que un traductor jurado traduzca los documentos.
She got her photo taken with the actor at the premiere. Se hizo una foto con el actor en el estreno.
We had the plumber fix the leak yesterday. Hicimos que el fontanero arreglara la fuga ayer.
I couldn't get my computer to start this morning. No conseguí que el ordenador arrancara esta mañana.
Where do you have your car serviced? ¿Dónde llevas el coche a hacer la revisión?
I'm going to have my teeth whitened before the wedding. Me voy a blanquear los dientes antes de la boda.

Pasiva con reporting verbs · Impersonal reporting

Cuando una opinión, creencia o rumor es compartido por mucha gente y no queremos (o no podemos) nombrar la fuente, usamos la pasiva con reporting verbs: say, believe, think, know, report, claim, expect, allege, rumour. Es el registro de prensa, informes y discurso académico.

📰Dos construcciones equivalentes: It is said that he lives in Paris ≡ He is said to live in Paris. La primera empieza con it impersonal; la segunda eleva el sujeto de la subordinada al principio (más elegante y compacta).
🔧
A. Estructuras · Impersonal passive patterns
Dos familias paralelas y cómo se relaciona el tiempo de la subordinada con el infinitivo.
Reporting verbs en pasiva — patrón y equivalencia temporal
EstructuraTiempo de la subordinadaEjemplo
It is said / believed / thought that + S + V Subordinada en presente (acción actual o habitual) It is said that he lives in Paris.
Se dice que vive en París.
It is said / believed that + S + V (past) Subordinada en pasado (acción ya terminada) It is believed that she left the country yesterday.
Se cree que abandonó el país ayer.
S + is said / believed / thought + to + V Acción simultánea a la del verbo principal (presente) He is said to live in Paris.
Se dice que vive en París.
S + is said / believed / thought + to have + V3 Acción anterior a la del verbo principal (pasado) She is said to have left the country yesterday.
Se dice que abandonó el país ayer.
S + is thought / believed + to be + V-ing Acción en curso ahora mismo The suspect is thought to be hiding abroad.
Se piensa que el sospechoso se está escondiendo en el extranjero.
S + was reported / rumoured + to + V Verbo principal en pasado: la creencia se sostuvo en su momento The minister was rumoured to be on the verge of resigning.
Se rumoreaba que el ministro estaba a punto de dimitir.
S + is expected / known / claimed + to + V Funciona igual con expect, know, claim, allege, understand, report The new law is expected to come into force in January.
Se espera que la nueva ley entre en vigor en enero.
There is said / believed + to be + N Cuando la subordinada activa empezaría por There is/are… There are believed to be over a thousand species at risk.
Se cree que hay más de mil especies en peligro.

E. En contexto · Passive reporting in real prose
Dos textos modelo (noticia + informe formal) con las pasivas resaltadas.

Model 1 · News article — Energy reform in Brussels

Style: formal news report · ~150 words · 8 passive structures

HeadlineNew energy policy unveiled in Brussels amid mounting criticism.

A controversial energy reform package was presented by the European Commission yesterday after months of negotiation. According to officials, the new measures are expected to come into force by January 2027, and several thousand jobs are believed to be at stake across the continent.

The proposal, which had been criticised by environmental groups before its publication, was eventually approved with minor amendments. It is said that the final draft was rewritten three times following pressure from member states.

The CEO of one of the largest utility providers, who is thought to have been informed in advance, declined to comment. Further details are due to be published next week, and the legislation will be debated in parliament throughout the autumn.

Model 2 · Academic / Business report extract

Style: impersonal academic register · ~135 words · 9 passive structures

FindingsThe survey was conducted between March and May 2026 and was completed by 1,240 participants across twelve regions. It is widely believed that remote work has reshaped office culture, and the data collected in this study appears to support that view.

Productivity is reported to have risen by 14 % among employees who work from home at least three days a week. However, levels of informal collaboration are said to have declined in the same group. Several recommendations have been put forward, including hybrid schemes and dedicated co-working hours.

A more detailed analysis will be presented in the next quarterly review, and the full dataset is expected to be made public by the end of the year.

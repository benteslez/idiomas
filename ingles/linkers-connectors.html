Adición y secuencia · Addition & Sequence

Sirven para sumar argumentos, ordenar ideas y construir párrafos cohesivos. Los adverbios conectivos (Moreover, Furthermore, In addition) abren oración con coma; las conjunciones (and, plus) enlazan elementos dentro de una misma oración.

🎯Linguaskill: Coherence & Cohesion es uno de los cuatro criterios de calificación en Writing P1 (email) y P2 (essay). Variar los conectores de adición sube directamente la nota.
🔗

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Errores típicos de adición y secuencia
!
"Moreover" no une dos oraciones con coma sola

Es adverbio, no conjunción: *The plan failed, moreover the costs rose → The plan failed. Moreover, the costs rose. O con punto y coma: The plan failed; moreover, the costs rose.

!
Coma obligatoria tras conector inicial

Moreover, …, Furthermore, …, In addition, …, Firstly, … Todos llevan coma. En español es opcional; en inglés es la norma escrita.

!
No empieces oración formal con "And"

En Writing P2, evita iniciar con And, But, So: usa Moreover, However, Therefore. En español "Y además" es válido; en inglés académico no.

!
"Además" no siempre es "moreover"

El español "además" puede ser moreover/furthermore (formal), also (neutro) o besides. Reserva moreover para añadir un argumento de peso, no cualquier idea adicional.

!
Orden de correlativos: "not only … but also"

El segundo elemento debe ir tras but also: *She is not only intelligent, also kind → She is not only intelligent but also kind. Si inicia oración, exige inversión: Not only does she sing, but she also dances.

!
"Firstly / Secondly / Finally" — no repitas patrón

No mezcles First con Secondly: o todos en -ly (Firstly, Secondly, Lastly) o todos sin él (First, Second, Last). La consistencia se evalúa.

Contraste y concesión · Contrast & Concession

Permiten introducir ideas opuestas o reconocer un argumento contrario. La distinción clave es gramatical: However / Nevertheless son adverbios (separan oraciones), Although / Even though son conjunciones (introducen cláusula), Despite / In spite of son preposiciones (van con sustantivo o gerundio).

🎯Linguaskill: el contraste es la función que más aparece en los huecos de Reading (Parts 2–4) y en Part 7 — Missing Sentences, donde la pista cohesiva (However, Nevertheless) decide la respuesta correcta.
🔗
A. Conectores por función
Subfunciones de contraste y concesión con ejemplos
Contraste directo (adverbio)sentence-linking
  • However, the results were inconclusive.
  • Nevertheless, the project went ahead.
  • Nonetheless, the team succeeded.
  • Still, the cost remains a concern.
Contraste por oposicióntwo sides
  • On the one hand, it is cheap; on the other hand, it is unreliable.
  • In contrast, the rival product lasts longer.
  • By contrast, rural areas grew faster.
  • Conversely, rates fell in the south.
Concesión (conjunción)+ clause
  • Although it rained, we went out.
  • Even though she was tired, she finished.
  • Though small, the team was efficient.
  • While / Whereas Spain exports oil, France imports it.
Concesión (preposición)+ noun · -ing
  • Despite the rain, we went out.
  • In spite of being tired, she finished.
  • Despite the fact that he was late, …
  • Regardless of the cost, …
Concesión enfáticastronger contrast
  • Yet, nobody complained.
  • Even so, the deal closed.
  • That said, exceptions remain.
  • All the same, we proceeded.
Contraste informalemail casual · speaking
  • But nobody came.
  • Though (al final) — It's cold. Nice though.
  • Mind you, it wasn't easy.
  • Then again, it could work.

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Errores típicos de contraste y concesión
!
"However" no une oraciones con coma (comma splice)

*It rained, however we went out ✗. Correcto: It rained. However, we went out. O con punto y coma: It rained; however, we went out. El español "sin embargo" sí admite la coma sola, pero el inglés no.

!
"Despite of" no existe

Es despite o in spite of, pero nunca *despite of. Lo más fácil: despite (una palabra) o in spite of (tres palabras) — sin solapar.

!
"Despite" + sustantivo o -ing, NUNCA + oración

*Despite he was late, … → Despite being late, … o Despite the fact that he was late, … o Although he was late, …. La preposición no admite cláusula con sujeto.

!
"Although … but" — un solo conector

No dupliques: *Although it rained, but we went out ✗. En inglés se usa although o but, no los dos. El error viene del español "aunque llovía, pero salimos".

!
"On the other hand" exige contraste previo

Si no has presentado un primer punto de vista, no puedes usarlo. O lo introduces con On the one hand, …, o sustitúyelo por However. *The film was bad. On the other hand, I left. ✗ — no hay dos lados.

!
"While / Whereas" expresan contraste, no tiempo

En posición inicial y con cláusulas paralelas, son contraste: Whereas Spain exports oil, France imports it. Si lo que quieres es "mientras" temporal, usa while con verbos progresivos: While I was reading, the phone rang.

!
No empieces oración formal con "But"

En Writing P2 sustituye But inicial por However, Yet o Nevertheless. But coordina dentro de la oración; al inicio de párrafo suena coloquial.

Causa, efecto y propósito · Cause, Effect & Purpose

Construyen la línea lógica de cualquier argumentación. Distingue siempre entre conjunción (because, since, as · + cláusula), preposición (because of, due to, owing to · + sustantivo), adverbio conectivo de efecto (therefore, consequently, thus) y propósito (in order to + infinitivo / so that + cláusula con modal).

🎯Linguaskill: esencial en Reading Part 7 (Extended Reading) y en la argumentación del essay (Writing P2). En Speaking P4, justificar opiniones con therefore / as a result demuestra control de B2+.
🔗
A. Conectores por función
Causa, efecto, propósito y consecuencia con ejemplos
Causa (conjunción)+ clause
  • Because the bridge collapsed, traffic was diverted.
  • Since nobody objected, we approved the plan.
  • As she was unwell, she stayed home.
  • Given that deadlines were tight, we acted fast.
  • Seeing that nobody complained, we proceeded.
Causa (preposición)+ noun / -ing
  • Because of the rain, the match was cancelled.
  • Due to technical issues, the train was late.
  • Owing to heavy snow, schools closed.
  • On account of her illness, she withdrew.
  • Thanks to his help, we finished early.
Efecto (adverbio)formal · essay
  • Therefore, the policy must be reviewed.
  • Consequently, profits fell.
  • Thus, the agreement was signed.
  • Hence, the new strategy.
  • Accordingly, measures were taken.
Efecto (frase)discourse linkers
  • As a result, sales dropped.
  • As a consequence, the law was amended.
  • For this reason, we postponed the meeting.
  • This led to a sharp decline.
  • This resulted in further delays.
Propósito (+ infinitivo)aim of action
  • In order to reduce costs, we restructured.
  • So as to avoid delays, we left early.
  • To save money, switch off the lights.
  • With the aim of boosting exports, …
Propósito (+ cláusula)+ modal verb
  • So that we can finish on time, …
  • In order that all members may attend, …
  • negativo · so as not to / in order not to + inf
  • precaución · in case it rains (≠ if)

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Errores típicos de causa, efecto y propósito
!
"Due to / Owing to" no van con oración

Son preposiciones: exigen sustantivo o gerundio. *Due to it rained, … ✗ → Due to the rain, … o Because it rained, …. Owing to being late, … con gerundio sí vale.

!
"Therefore" es adverbio: cuidado con comma splice

*Sales dropped, therefore we changed strategy ✗. Correcto: Sales dropped. Therefore, we changed strategy. O con punto y coma: Sales dropped; therefore, we changed strategy.

!
"So that" exige modal en la cláusula

Para propósito futuro, usa can / will; para hipotético o pasado, could / would: I left early so that I could catch the train. Sin modal, suena raro: *so that I catch the train.

!
"As" tiene tres valores: causa, tiempo y comparación

(1) Causa: As I was tired, I left. (2) Tiempo: As I was leaving, the phone rang. (3) Comparación: As big as a house. El contexto desambigua — al escribir, prefiere since/because para causa si hay riesgo de ambigüedad.

!
"So" inicial es informal

En essay (Writing P2) sustituye So, sales fell por Therefore, sales fell o As a result, sales fell. En español "así que" es neutro; so al inicio en inglés escrito formal baja registro.

!
"In order to" + infinitivo, "in order that" + cláusula

*In order to we save money ✗ → In order to save money, … o In order that we may save money, …. Mismo sujeto = infinitivo; sujeto distinto = cláusula con modal.

!
"Because of" ≠ "because"

Because of = preposición (because of the rain); because = conjunción (because it rained). Confundirlos es un fallo típico en huecos de Reading.

Énfasis, ejemplo y resumen · Emphasis, Example & Summary

Cierran el inventario discursivo: enfatizar (indeed, in fact, above all), ejemplificar (for instance, such as, namely) y concluir (in conclusion, to sum up, overall). Son los conectores que más matiz aportan en un essay y los que más diferencian un B2 sólido de un C1.

🎯Linguaskill: imprescindibles en el último párrafo del essay (Writing P2) — In conclusion, … Overall, … — y en Speaking P4 para concretar con ejemplos: For instance, in my country, …
🔗

  • In fact, the figures doubled in a year.

  • More importantly, jobs were created.
Ejemploillustrating
  • For instance, in Germany, recycling rates are high.
  • For example, consider the case of Sweden.

  • In other words, the plan failed.
  • i.e. the working population (paréntesis)
  • Or rather, half the population.

  • In conclusion, the policy should continue.
  • To sum up, the benefits outweigh the costs.
  • To conclude, further research is needed.

  • At the end of the day, … (informal)

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Errores típicos de énfasis, ejemplo y resumen
!
"Actually" no es "actualmente"

Actually = en realidad / de hecho, no "actualmente". Para "actualmente" usa currently, at present, nowadays. *Actually I live in Madrid (= "En realidad vivo en Madrid") ≠ "Actualmente vivo en Madrid" → Currently / At present, I live in Madrid.

!
"For example" + oración / "such as" + lista nominal

For example, Germany has cut emissions (oración completa). Countries such as Germany and France (lista nominal). No mezcles: *Such as Germany has cut emissions ✗.

!
"e.g." vs "i.e." — significados distintos

e.g. (exempli gratia) = "por ejemplo", introduce ejemplos no exhaustivos: EU countries (e.g., Spain, France). i.e. (id est) = "es decir", introduce aclaraciones exactas: the EU-27 (i.e., the current members). En texto formal van entre paréntesis.

!
"In conclusion" solo en el último párrafo

Anuncia el cierre del texto: úsalo una sola vez, al inicio del último párrafo del essay. En un email corto suena pomposo — sustitúyelo por Overall, All in all o simplemente cierra con la idea principal.

!
No repitas literalmente en la conclusión

El error típico tras In conclusion, es copiar la tesis con otras palabras. Suma una valoración final, una recomendación o una proyección: In conclusion, the evidence supports investment; further studies should now focus on cost-benefit analysis.

!
"Like" como "such as" suena informal en escritura

Cities like Madrid es aceptable en speaking, pero en Writing P2 prefiere Cities such as Madrid. Algunos manuales aún lo penalizan en registro académico estricto.

!
"Above all" enfatiza la idea más importante, no la suma

No es sinónimo de moreover. Marca la idea principal dentro de varios argumentos: The plan is cheap, quick and, above all, safe. Si solo añades, usa moreover o furthermore.

E. En contexto · Connectors in action
Párrafos modelo con los conectores coloreados por función

Model 1 · Essay paragraph — Public transport investment

Writing P2 · ~165 words · all four functions in play

SeqFirst and foremost, well-funded public transport reduces the dependence on private cars, which in turn lowers urban congestion and pollution. Moreover, frequent and affordable services give low-income citizens access to jobs and education, in addition to easing the daily routines of millions of commuters.

ContrastHowever, opponents argue that the financial cost is prohibitive, especially in smaller cities with low ridership. Nevertheless, this objection overlooks the long-term savings in healthcare and environmental damage. Indeed, several European studies have shown that every euro invested in metro and bus systems generates roughly two euros in economic activity.

CauseTherefore, governments should view public transport not as an expense but as a strategic investment. In conclusion, although the initial outlay is considerable, the social, economic and environmental benefits more than justify the policy.

Model 2 · Essay paragraph — Remote work

Writing P2 · ~165 words · emphasis, example & summary in focus

TopicRemote work has reshaped the labour market in ways few would have predicted a decade ago. In fact, recent surveys suggest that over half of European employees now work from home at least one day a week. Furthermore, companies report measurable gains in productivity and lower office costs.

ExamplesThe benefits are visible across very different sectors. For instance, technology firms such as Microsoft and Spotify have adopted hybrid policies as a permanent standard. On the other hand, traditional industries — namely manufacturing and hospitality — cannot easily follow the same path.

ClosureAll in all, the trend appears irreversible. In conclusion, although remote work is not a universal solution, its careful integration is, above all, a sign of how flexible the modern workplace must become.

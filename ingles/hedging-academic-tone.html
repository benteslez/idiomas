Qué es el hedging · What is hedging?

El hedging consiste en suavizar afirmaciones con modales, adverbios y verbos específicos para sonar mesurado, objetivo y académico. Un texto C1 toma postura clara, pero rara vez formula sus tesis como verdades absolutas: las matiza para reconocer la complejidad del tema y la posibilidad de excepciones.

🎓Linguaskill C1: el hedging es el rasgo nº 1 que el examinador asocia al academic tone en el Essay (Writing Part 2). En el Speaking Part 4 (evaluación de argumentos), un par de matizadores bien colocados (tend to, may, is likely to) elevan la nota de range sin que el discurso pierda fuerza.

A. Concepto · La misma idea, con y sin hedging
Afirmación tajante vs versión matizada — el cambio de tono lo dice todo
Tajante: Technology has destroyed face-to-face communication.
Matizado: Technology may have weakened face-to-face communication in some contexts. de absoluto a tendencia
Tajante: All teenagers are addicted to social media.
Matizado: Many teenagers appear to spend considerable time on social media. cuantificador acotado + verbo de apariencia
Tajante: Working from home is better than the office.
Matizado: Working from home tends to offer significant advantages for many employees. tend to + cuantificador
Tajante: Climate change will destroy the planet.
Matizado: Climate change is likely to have severe consequences if current trends continue. probabilidad + condicionalidad
Tajante: Nobody reads books anymore.
Matizado: There appears to be a decline in reading habits among younger generations. there appears to be + restricción del alcance
Tajante: Capitalism causes inequality.
Matizado: It could be argued that capitalism contributes significantly to economic inequality. expresión impersonal + contribución parcial
Tajante: Everyone agrees that exercise is essential.
Matizado: It is widely acknowledged that regular exercise tends to be beneficial. consenso descrito, no postulado
Tajante: AI will replace all jobs.
Matizado: AI is likely to transform many sectors, though the extent of job replacement remains uncertain. reconoce el desacuerdo

D. Trampas para hispanohablantes
Calcos del español que delatan B1/B2 cuando apuntas a C1
!
Evita los absolutos categóricos

Adverbios como always, never, everyone, nobody, all y none son verdaderos imanes para el lector crítico: una sola excepción tira abajo tu argumento. Sustitúyelos por tend to, many, most, few, in most cases, generally, typically. En un essay C1, una afirmación absoluta sin matizar suena ingenua.

!
No abuses de "I think" / "In my opinion"

El español académico permite "creo que / pienso que"; el inglés académico C1 prefiere mover el yo al fondo: It could be argued that…, It seems that…, The evidence suggests that…, There is a strong case for…. Reserva I would argue para uno o dos momentos clave del essay.

!
"Tend to" + infinitivo, no gerundio

Calcado del español "tienden a hacer": ✗ Students tend to studying online. → ✓ Students tend to study online. Es el verbo de generalización académica por excelencia y conviene tenerlo automatizado.

!
El español también matiza, pero con otras herramientas

En español usamos "podría", "parece que", "en cierta medida". El inglés académico añade un repertorio léxico específico (appear to, suggest, indicate, is likely to) y formas impersonales (it is widely acknowledged) que conviene transferir conscientemente, no traducir literalmente.

!
Matizar ≠ diluir la tesis

Un essay C1 toma postura con claridad — el hedging la viste de cautela, no la borra. Si todas las frases dicen "perhaps", "to some extent", "in some cases", el examinador pensará que no sabes qué defender. Receta sana: 1-2 matizadores por párrafo + una afirmación nítida que sostenga la tesis.

!
"Probably" frente a "is likely to"

En conversación, probably funciona. En un essay, is likely to + infinitivo suena más académico: ✓ Remote work is likely to remain widespread. > Remote work will probably remain widespread. Cambio mínimo, salto inmediato de registro.

Recursos para matizar · The Hedging Toolkit

El kit del hedging académico se organiza en cuatro categorías: modales (may, might, could), adverbios (perhaps, possibly, arguably), verbos (suggest, indicate, appear, tend to) y expresiones prefabricadas (it could be argued that, there is a tendency to, to some extent). Combinar dos categorías en una misma frase es la firma del tono C1.

🧰Linguaskill C1: en un essay de 250 palabras, intenta incluir al menos un elemento de cada categoría (modal + adverbio + verbo + expresión). Demuestra grammar range y vocabulario académico sin sonar artificial. Eso sí: no repitas dos veces el mismo matizador.

A. Por categoría · Four toolkits
Modales · adverbios · verbos · expresiones
Modalesmodal verbs
  • may / might / could — posibilidad
  • can — capacidad teórica, no compromiso
  • would — hipótesis cortés
  • This may explain the rise in inequality.
  • Such a policy could backfire.
Adverbiosadverbs
  • perhaps / possibly / arguably — duda razonable
  • presumably / seemingly / apparently — apariencia
  • allegedly — atribución no verificada
  • generally / typically / in some cases / to some extent — cuantificación cauta
  • Arguably, the most pressing issue is housing.
Verbosverbs
  • suggest / indicate / imply / point to — evidencia parcial
  • appear / seem — apariencia, no certeza
  • tend to + infinitivo — generalización
  • is likely / unlikely to — probabilidad
  • The data suggest that reform is overdue.
Expresionesprefab phrases
  • It could be argued that…
  • There is a tendency to / for…
  • The evidence suggests that…
  • This would imply that…
  • To some extent / In some respects…
  • It is widely acknowledged that…

C. Ejemplos en frase · Hedging in real sentences
Cada frase combina al menos una categoría · EN + ES
Heavy use of social media may contribute to anxiety in adolescents. El uso intensivo de redes sociales puede contribuir a la ansiedad en adolescentes.
Perhaps the most striking finding is the sharp rise in remote work since 2020. Quizás el hallazgo más llamativo sea el fuerte aumento del teletrabajo desde 2020.
It could be argued that higher taxes discourage entrepreneurship. Podría sostenerse que los impuestos altos desincentivan el emprendimiento.
Critics tend to focus on the short-term costs of the policy. Los críticos suelen centrarse en los costes a corto plazo de la política.
The data suggest that there is a strong correlation between exercise and mental wellbeing. Los datos sugieren una fuerte correlación entre el ejercicio y el bienestar mental.
Universities appear to be adapting more slowly than employers to the AI revolution. Las universidades parecen estar adaptándose más despacio que los empleadores a la revolución de la IA.
Arguably, public transport should be subsidised in order to reduce urban pollution. Podría argumentarse que el transporte público debería subvencionarse para reducir la contaminación urbana.
There is a tendency for first-time voters to favour smaller parties. Existe una tendencia entre los votantes primerizos a inclinarse por partidos minoritarios.
The decline in birth rates is likely to strain public pension systems. Es probable que la caída de la natalidad ponga bajo presión los sistemas públicos de pensiones.
To some extent, this problem can be addressed through education and public-awareness campaigns. Hasta cierto punto, este problema puede abordarse mediante la educación y las campañas de concienciación.
Recent studies indicate that hybrid work models may outperform fully remote arrangements. Estudios recientes indican que los modelos híbridos podrían superar al teletrabajo total. (verbo + modal en la misma frase)
It is widely acknowledged that early childhood education tends to yield long-term benefits. Se reconoce ampliamente que la educación temprana tiende a generar beneficios a largo plazo. (expresión + verbo)

Cautela y objetividad · Distanced Stance

Dos movimientos separan un essay C1 de uno B2: despersonalizar la voz (mover el yo al fondo con it could be argued, it seems, the evidence suggests) y cuantificar con cautela (sustituir absolutos como always / never / everyone por many, several, in some cases, to a certain extent). El resultado: una voz mesurada que el examinador asocia a nivel C1.

⚖️Linguaskill C1: el examinador busca pruebas de distanced stance. Una sola estructura impersonal por párrafo suele bastar para enviar la señal correcta; cuatro o cinco suenan a plantilla aprendida. El objetivo es parecer académico, no rígido.

A. Despersonalizar · From "I" to "It"
Reescritura sistemática de estructuras personales y absolutas
Coloquial / categóricoAcadémico / matizadoFunción
I think social media is bad for teenagers.It could be argued that social media may have adverse effects on adolescents.opinión personal → análisis académico
Everyone knows that climate change is real.It is widely acknowledged that climate change poses a significant challenge.generalización absoluta → consenso descrito
We all agree that pollution is a problem.There appears to be broad consensus that pollution represents a pressing concern.afirmación grupal → consenso descrito impersonalmente
Studies show that exercise works.Studies tend to indicate that exercise has measurable benefits.verbo tajante → tendencia descrita
Nobody can deny that AI is changing the world.Few would deny that AI is reshaping many areas of contemporary life.absoluto retórico → cautela explícita
I believe remote work is the future.It seems that / It would appear that remote work is likely to play a growing role in the labour market.yo personal → voz impersonal + probabilidad
All teenagers are addicted to their phones.Many teenagers tend to spend considerable time on their phones.universal → cuantificación matizada
This is the best solution.This may well be the most effective solution available.superlativo absoluto → superlativo matizado
It always fails.It frequently fails / It tends to fail under such conditions.adverbio absoluto → adverbio matizado

E. En contexto · Before / After hedging
Párrafos de essay tajantes reescritos con hedging · matizadores resaltados en azul

Model 1 · Social Media & Mental Health

Body paragraph · before vs after hedging · ~130 words

BeforeSocial media is destroying the mental health of an entire generation. Teenagers are addicted to their phones, scroll all day and never interact face-to-face. This explains why anxiety rates have exploded. The only solution is to ban social media for under-16s. Anyone who disagrees is ignoring the evidence.

AfterSocial media may be contributing to a decline in adolescent wellbeing. It is widely acknowledged that many young people tend to spend considerable time on these platforms, and this could partly explain the marked rise in anxiety reported in recent years. It could be argued that restricting access for under-16s would help to mitigate these effects, although it remains uncertain whether such measures would prove effective in practice.

Model 2 · Working from Home

Body paragraph · before vs after hedging · ~125 words

BeforeWorking from home is always better than going to the office. Everyone is more productive at home, saves a fortune on commuting and enjoys a far better quality of life. Anyone who still prefers the office is clearly out of touch with modern work culture.

AfterWorking from home appears to offer significant advantages for many employees, particularly in terms of flexibility and reduced commuting costs. The evidence suggests that a considerable proportion of workers report higher productivity, although this may not apply uniformly across sectors. It could be argued that hybrid models, which combine the benefits of both, represent a more sustainable long-term solution for most organisations.

Model 3 · AI & Employment

Body paragraph · before vs after hedging · ~140 words

BeforeAI will destroy millions of jobs. Workers in every sector are doomed. Governments must ban AI immediately or the consequences will be catastrophic. Anyone who claims otherwise is in denial.

AfterRecent advances in artificial intelligence are likely to transform many sectors of the labour market. The extent to which these changes will displace human workers remains a matter of debate, although some studies suggest that roles involving routine cognitive tasks appear to be particularly exposed. Arguably, the most effective response would involve a combination of regulation, reskilling and social safety nets, rather than outright prohibition, since blanket bans tend to stifle innovation without addressing the underlying labour-market shifts.

Conectores para el essay argumentativo

Un essay bien escrito alterna conectores básicos con variantes más sofisticadas. Evita el abuso de firstly / secondly / finally: en Cambridge B2–C1 se valora la variedad léxica y el registro académico. Usa cada conector con un objetivo claro: introducir, añadir, contrastar, ejemplificar, opinar, razonar o concluir.

🚪
Introducir ideas
Alternativas elegantes a Firstly / Secondly
To begin withpara empezar
Apertura neutra y elegante de un primer argumento.
To begin with, there is overwhelming evidence that social media affects sleep patterns.
At first glancea primera vista
Útil para introducir una idea que luego matizarás.
At first glance, remote work seems ideal, but a closer look reveals serious drawbacks.
It is often argued that…a menudo se argumenta que
Voz pasiva impersonal: introduce una postura ajena sin comprometerte.
It is often argued that stricter laws would reduce crime rates.
➕
Añadir ideas
Para sumar un argumento al anterior
Furthermoreademás
Formal. Añade información que refuerza la anterior.
Furthermore, the policy has been shown to reduce unemployment significantly.
Moreoveres más
Sinónimo elegante de furthermore; típico del registro académico.
Moreover, recent studies confirm the long-term benefits of bilingual education.
What is morelo que es más
Ligeramente menos formal; añade énfasis.
The system is expensive. What is more, it does not even work properly.
In additionademás, adicionalmente
Conector neutro y versátil; también: in addition to + nombre/gerundio.
In addition, the new measures aim to protect small businesses.
⚖️
Contrastar
Introducir contraargumentos y oposiciones
Howeversin embargo
Conector de contraste más usado en escritura formal. Va seguido de coma.
Online learning offers flexibility. However, it lacks human interaction.
Neverthelessno obstante
Más enfático que however. Sinónimo: nonetheless.
The project was costly. Nevertheless, the results justified the investment.
Although / Even thoughaunque
Conjunciones + oración completa. Even though es más enfático.
Although the law was passed, it has rarely been enforced.
Despite / In spite ofa pesar de
+ nombre o gerundio, nunca + oración.
Despite the warnings, many people ignored the regulations.
While it is true that…si bien es cierto que
Concesión académica: reconoces el otro lado antes de refutarlo.
While it is true that technology can isolate us, it also connects distant families.
Whereasmientras que
Compara dos elementos contrarios en la misma oración.
Some prioritise career growth, whereas others value work-life balance.
💡
Dar ejemplos
Concretar argumentos con evidencia
For instancepor ejemplo
Alternativa formal y elegante a for example.
Many cities have adopted green policies. For instance, Copenhagen aims to be carbon-neutral by 2025.
For examplepor ejemplo
Neutro y universal; el más versátil.
Some skills are essential. For example, critical thinking and communication are key.
Such ascomo, tales como
Integra ejemplos dentro de la oración, sin pausa.
Renewable sources such as solar and wind energy are gaining ground.
A clear example of this is…un claro ejemplo es
Sintagma completo: ideal para abrir párrafo de evidencia.
A clear example of this is the success of Scandinavian welfare systems.
This can be seen in…esto se ve en
Frase pasiva impersonal, propia del registro académico.
This can be seen in the rising number of remote workers worldwide.
💬
Expresar opinión (avanzada)
Más allá de I think / In my opinion
It could be argued that…podría argumentarse que
Cauteloso y académico; presenta una postura como debatible.
It could be argued that early education is the foundation of social mobility.
From my perspectivedesde mi punto de vista
Personal pero formal.
From my perspective, investing in renewable energy is the only viable path.
In my viewa mi parecer
Más elegante que in my opinion; mismo significado.
In my view, the benefits clearly outweigh the disadvantages.
Personally, I believe that…personalmente, creo que
Marca la opinión como propia, con cierto énfasis.
Personally, I believe that education should remain free and accessible.
I am convinced that…estoy convencido de que
Alto grado de certeza: úsalo en la tesis o la conclusión.
I am convinced that these reforms will benefit future generations.
There is a strong case for…hay sólidos argumentos a favor de
Forma impersonal y muy académica. + nombre/gerundio.
There is a strong case for raising the minimum wage in this sector.
⚡
Causa
Por qué algo ocurre
Due todebido a
+ nombre o gerundio. No con oración completa.
The event was cancelled due to bad weather.
Owing toa causa de
Sinónimo formal de due to. + nombre/gerundio.
Owing to rising costs, many small firms have closed.
This is largely becauseesto se debe en gran parte a que
Conector explicativo + oración. Suaviza con largely.
Cities are becoming overcrowded. This is largely because rural opportunities are disappearing.
🎯
Consecuencia
Lo que se deriva de lo anterior
Thereforepor lo tanto
El conector causal más estándar en escritura formal.
The data is incomplete; therefore, the conclusions remain tentative.
As a resultcomo resultado
Resultado directo de una causa concreta.
The currency lost value. As a result, imports became unaffordable.
Consequentlyen consecuencia
Más formal y enfático que therefore.
Funding was withdrawn. Consequently, the project was abandoned.
For this reasonpor esta razón
Apunta a una causa previa específica.
The system is outdated. For this reason, a full reform is necessary.
🔀
Condición
Hipótesis y requisitos
Ifsi
Condicional neutro. Combínalo con tipos 1, 2, 3.
If governments invested more in schools, literacy would improve.
Unlessa menos que
= if…not. Negativo implícito.
Unless action is taken, the situation will worsen.
As long assiempre que, mientras
Condición necesaria. Sinónimo: so long as.
Progress is possible as long as citizens remain engaged.
Whethersi (o no), tanto si
Introduce dos posibilidades. A menudo: whether…or.
Whether we like it or not, climate change affects us all.
Provided thatsiempre que, a condición de que
Muy formal. Sinónimo: providing that.
The plan will work, provided that all parties cooperate.
🔄
Reformulación
Aclarar o precisar lo dicho
In other wordsen otras palabras
Reformula una idea de forma más sencilla o clara.
The proposal lacks empirical support. In other words, there is no real evidence behind it.
More specificallymás concretamente
Pasa de lo general a lo concreto: añade precisión.
The reform targets vulnerable groups. More specifically, it focuses on single-parent families.
🔥
Intensificación
Reforzar una afirmación
Indeedde hecho, ciertamente
Confirma y refuerza la idea anterior con énfasis formal.
The strategy is risky; indeed, several experts have warned against it.
In factde hecho, en realidad
Introduce un dato real que reafirma o contradice lo previo.
The policy seems controversial. In fact, it enjoys broad public support.
Moreover / What is moreademás, es más
También funcionan como intensificadores cuando refuerzan el argumento previo.
The plan is unfair. What is more, it is economically unviable.
🏁
Concluir
Cierre del ensayo con propósito
To sum upen resumen
Cierre práctico y claro. Sinónimo: in summary.
To sum up, both approaches have merit, but balance is essential.
All things consideredconsiderándolo todo
Conclusión ponderada, tras valorar pros y contras.
All things considered, the benefits clearly outweigh the disadvantages.
Taking everything into accountteniéndolo todo en cuenta
Variante elegante de all things considered; ideal para C1.
Taking everything into account, a balanced policy seems the most sensible solution.
Ultimatelyen última instancia
Marca el fondo del asunto: lo verdaderamente importante.
Ultimately, the decision rests with the citizens themselves.
On balanceen conjunto, ponderándolo
Muy típico del essay Cambridge: cierre equilibrado.
On balance, the advantages of remote work outweigh its drawbacks.

Adverbios para el essay

El uso preciso de adverbios eleva el registro y demuestra control léxico. Sirven para enfatizar, matizar, marcar frecuencia, expresar certeza o duda. Combínalos con verbos modales y estructuras hipotéticas para conseguir un tono académico.

🔥
Enfatizar ideas
Adverbios de grado e intensidad
Highlyaltamente
Con adjetivos abstractos: highly relevant, highly controversial, highly likely.
This is a highly controversial topic in modern politics.
Extremelyextremadamente
Grado máximo. Combina con adjetivos graduables (important, rare).
The findings are extremely significant for future research.
Particularlyparticularmente
Destaca un caso entre otros similares.
This issue is particularly relevant for young adults.
Remarkablynotablemente
Llama la atención sobre algo sorprendente.
The number of users has grown remarkably in just five years.
Significantlysignificativamente
Estadísticamente relevante o de impacto considerable.
Emissions have dropped significantly since 2015.
Considerablyconsiderablemente
Cuantitativo. Sinónimo: substantially.
Costs have risen considerably over the past decade.
🪶
Matizar (hedging)
Suavizar afirmaciones · típico del registro académico
Apparentlyaparentemente
Lo que parece ser cierto sin confirmación total.
Apparently, the new system works better in small businesses.
Arguablypodría argumentarse que
Clave en C1: introduce una afirmación fuerte de forma cauta.
Shakespeare is arguably the greatest writer in the English language.
Presumablysupuestamente, presumiblemente
Deducción razonable basada en evidencia.
The minister, presumably aware of the criticism, refused to comment.
To some extenthasta cierto punto
Acepta parcialmente una idea sin comprometerse totalmente.
To some extent, social media has improved global communication.
Partiallyparcialmente
Algo es verdad solo en parte.
The reform has been partially successful in rural areas.
Relativelyrelativamente
En comparación con algo implícito.
The procedure is relatively simple and inexpensive.
📊
Frecuencia
Cuán a menudo ocurre algo
Widelyampliamente
Extensión geográfica o social. Frecuente con known, used, believed, accepted.
This theory is widely accepted in the scientific community.
Rarelyraramente
Negativo. Puede provocar inversión al inicio.
Rarely have we faced such a complex challenge.
Constantlyconstantemente
Acción continua, a veces con matiz crítico.
The technology sector is constantly evolving.
Frequentlyfrecuentemente
Sinónimo formal de often.
Students frequently complain about the workload.
Commonlycomúnmente
De forma habitual o típica.
This term is commonly used in academic writing.
Increasinglycada vez más
Muy útil en essays: marca tendencias en aumento.
Companies are increasingly adopting flexible work policies.
✅
Certeza
Afirmar con seguridad
Undoubtedlysin duda
Grado máximo de certeza, registro alto.
Climate change is undoubtedly the defining challenge of our age.
Certainlyciertamente
Refuerza una afirmación; menos enfático que undoubtedly.
The plan will certainly generate controversy.
Clearlyclaramente
Marca una conclusión evidente. Ideal en argumentación.
The results clearly indicate a need for reform.
❓
Duda
Probabilidad y conjetura
Probablyprobablemente
Probabilidad alta (~80%).
The economy will probably recover next year.
Possiblyposiblemente
Posibilidad menor (~50%).
The policy possibly needs further revision.
Presumablypresumiblemente
Deducción lógica a partir de evidencia disponible.
The author, presumably influenced by his upbringing, focuses on rural themes.
Apparentlyaparentemente
Lo que se observa o se ha dicho, sin garantía.
Apparently, the original proposal was rejected by the committee.

Estructuras gramaticales para el essay

Las estructuras avanzadas son el sello del C1: concesivas, relativas, condicionales, pasivas, comparativas, inversiones y oraciones de participio. Úsalas con intención — una sola inversión bien colocada vale más que diez frases simples.

💡Consejo Cambridge: los examinadores valoran range of structures. Intenta incluir al menos una pasiva, una concesiva, una condicional y una de participio en cada essay.
⚖️
Concesivas
Conceder una idea antes de matizarla o refutarla

Although / Even though + oración

Conjunción + sujeto + verbo. Even though es más enfático.

Although + S + V, main clause
  • Although the policy was unpopular, it proved effective.
  • Even though she lacked experience, she was promoted.

While it is true that…

Concesión académica: reconoces el otro lado.

While it is true that + S + V, …
  • While it is true that the cost is high, the long-term benefits justify it.

Despite / In spite of + nombre/gerundio

¡Atención! Nunca con oración completa.

Despite + noun / -ing, …
  • Despite the warnings, no action was taken.
  • In spite of being exhausted, he completed the task.
  • Despite the fact that + oración (única forma de usarlo con S+V).
🔗
Relativas
Pronombres relativos avanzados

which

Cosas, ideas o cláusulas completas (non-defining).

  • The reform, which was passed last year, has divided public opinion.
  • He arrived late, which annoyed everyone. (refiere a toda la cláusula)

whose

Posesivo. Para personas, cosas o instituciones.

  • Students whose grades fall below the threshold must retake the exam.
  • A company whose values align with sustainability is more attractive.

whereby

= "mediante el cual / por el que". Formal y muy académico.

  • A system whereby citizens can vote online is being developed.
  • The procedure whereby applicants are selected is transparent.

in which

Preposición + relativo, registro formal. Sustituye a where en contextos abstractos.

  • The era in which we live demands constant adaptation.
  • There are many cases in which this rule does not apply.
🔀
Condicionales
Hipótesis y consecuencias

First Conditional

Real / probable en el futuro.

If + present, will + infinitive
  • If people recycle more, the planet will benefit.

Second Conditional

Hipotético en presente o futuro (irreal).

If + past, would + infinitive
  • If governments invested more, education would improve.
  • Variante formal: If people were to change their habits…

Third Conditional

Hipotético en el pasado (irreal).

If + past perfect, would have + past participle
  • If they had acted sooner, the crisis would have been avoided.

If people were to…

Variante formal del second conditional, frecuente en essays.

If + S + were to + infinitive, would…
  • If governments were to ban single-use plastics, pollution would decrease.

Provided that / As long as

Condición necesaria. Muy formal el primero.

  • The plan will succeed, provided that all parties cooperate.
  • You may attend as long as you register in advance.
🔄
Pasivas
Voz pasiva impersonal · típica del registro académico

is considered

Presentar una opinión como aceptada sin atribuirla a nadie.

  • This novel is considered a masterpiece of modern literature.

is believed (to)

Construcción impersonal con infinitivo: muy académica.

It is believed that + S + V
S is believed to + inf.
  • It is believed that the changes will take effect next year.
  • The artefact is believed to date back to 500 BC.

has been criticised

Pasiva en present perfect: indica acción reciente o continuada.

  • The policy has been criticised by environmental groups.
  • His approach has been widely questioned in academic circles.

Otras pasivas útiles

Repertorio académico para variar.

  • is argued / claimed / suggested / reported / acknowledged
  • It is widely accepted that vaccines save lives.
  • This issue has been overlooked for too long.
📏
Comparativas
Comparaciones avanzadas y proporcionales

Far more / less… than

Comparativo intensificado.

  • This solution is far more sustainable than the previous one.
  • Critics are far less common than supporters.

By far the most / the least

Superlativo enfático.

  • This is by far the most important reform of the decade.
  • It was by far the least effective campaign in history.

nowhere near as… as

"Ni de lejos tan… como". Comparativo negativo intensificado.

  • The new system is nowhere near as efficient as expected.

The more… the more…

Comparativos proporcionales (correlación).

The more + S + V, the more + S + V
  • The more we invest in education, the more we benefit as a society.
  • The less we waste, the fewer resources we exhaust.

rather than

"En vez de". + nombre, gerundio o infinitivo (paralelo).

  • We should focus on prevention rather than cure.
  • Many prefer to walk rather than drive short distances.
🔁
Inversiones
Estructura enfática típica de C1

Not only… but also…

Con inversión obligatoria tras not only al inicio.

Not only + aux + S + V, but S also + V
  • Not only does the policy reduce emissions, but it also creates jobs.
  • Not only is this idea innovative, but it is also economically viable.

Otras inversiones útiles

Adverbios negativos al inicio fuerzan inversión.

  • Rarely have we faced such a complex challenge.
  • Hardly had they arrived when the meeting began.
  • Under no circumstances should this be ignored.
  • Only by working together can we succeed.
📐
Oraciones de participio
Cláusulas reducidas para condensar información

Having + past participle

Acción previa a la principal. Misma sujeto en ambas cláusulas.

Having + past participle, S + V
  • Having considered all the options, the board approved the plan.
  • Having lived abroad for years, she speaks five languages fluently.

-ing / -ed participles

Forma compacta para causa, tiempo o resultado.

  • Faced with declining sales, the company restructured.
  • Knowing the risks, they still proceeded.
  • Encouraged by the results, the team continued.
🪶
Matizar (estructuras)
Suavizar afirmaciones sin perder argumento

It could be argued that…

Marca una opinión como discutible. Muy académico.

  • It could be argued that economic growth has come at an environmental cost.

Tend to + infinitive

Tendencia general, no afirmación absoluta.

  • Young people tend to adopt new technologies more quickly.
  • Critics tend to overlook the long-term benefits.

Appear / Seem to + infinitive

Algo parece ser cierto, sin afirmarlo categóricamente.

  • The data appears to support this hypothesis.
  • The two phenomena seem to be closely related.

May / Might + infinitive

Posibilidad moderada. Might = ligeramente menos probable.

  • The reform may have unintended consequences.
  • This approach might not be suitable for every context.
